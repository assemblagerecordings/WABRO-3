# Personal Housing Support Summary

Private document. Share only with trusted support, Citizens Advice, Shelter, a solicitor, or a relevant housing adviser.

## What Help Is Needed

Luke needs urgent UK housing advice about leaving a property after a serious fire incident involving another occupier, and about disputed rent, cleaning/redecoration charges, and deposit deductions.

Luke is also dealing with mental health difficulties and may need clear, practical, step-by-step support rather than vague advice.

## Basic Situation

- Luke has been living at the property for around 3 years.
- There is a signed written agreement saying Luke can live there until 1 December 2026.
- Luke has paid rent reliably during the time living there.
- Luke has had to leave the property after a serious fire incident involving another occupier.
- Fire service and police attended the incident.
- Luke says there is photo/video evidence and evidence of emergency services attending.
- Luke is currently staying temporarily with his girlfriend and her family while looking for a 2 bedroom apartment.
- A moving van is booked for Wednesday 3 June 2026.

## Money / Demands In Dispute

The landlady is reportedly asking for:

- Rent for the current month.
- Payment for a painter.
- Payment for carpet cleaning.
- Retention of the deposit.

Luke disputes liability for these additional charges.

Luke says the only damage to the room is a few screw holes in the walls.

## Key Questions For Adviser

1. Is Luke legally a lodger, occupier with basic protection, tenant, or another status?
2. Does the written agreement until 1 December 2026 affect what rent is owed after leaving due to the fire incident?
3. What should Luke pay or refuse to pay while the dispute is unresolved?
4. What evidence should Luke collect before fully leaving?
5. Can the landlady deduct painting/carpet cleaning/deposit without itemised evidence?
6. What should Luke write to the landlady now?
7. What should Luke avoid saying or doing?
8. If the property was not properly declared or managed, who should that be reported to, and when?

## Evidence To Gather

- Signed agreement.
- Rent payment proof.
- Deposit payment proof.
- Messages from landlady about rent, deposit, cleaning, painter, and move-out.
- Photo/video evidence of the fire incident.
- Any emergency service reference numbers if available.
- Photos/videos of the room before leaving.
- Photos/videos of any screw holes or alleged damage.
- Moving van booking proof.
- Any evidence about who lives at the property and whether the landlady lives there.

## Calm Message To Landlady

Use written communication where possible.

Suggested message:

```text
I am vacating the property following the serious fire incident involving another occupier, which required attendance from the fire service and police.

I dispute liability for the additional charges requested. Please provide any claim for rent, cleaning, painting, carpet cleaning, damage, or deposit deductions in writing, with itemised costs and supporting evidence.

I will arrange removal of my belongings on Wednesday 3 June 2026. Please keep all communication in writing.
```

## Important Caution

Do not threaten the landlady about tax, reporting, or illegality in the first message. Keep that information for Citizens Advice, Shelter, the local council, or a solicitor if they advise it is relevant.

## Mental Health Safety

If Luke feels at immediate risk of harming himself or cannot stay safe:

- Call 999
- Go to A&E
- Call Samaritans on 116 123 in the UK

The immediate practical priorities are:

1. Stay physically safe.
2. Move belongings safely.
3. Keep everything in writing.
4. Photograph and document the room.
5. Get proper housing advice before escalating the dispute.
